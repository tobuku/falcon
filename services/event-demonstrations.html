What Is a Falconry Demonstration?

A falconry demonstration is a live presentation where a licensed falconer showcases trained raptors — typically hawks, falcons, or owls — to an audience. Demonstrations can include free-flight displays, bird handling, educational talks about raptor biology, and interactive experiences where attendees can hold a bird on a gloved hand.

These events are led by falconers who hold USDA exhibition permits and carry liability insurance. The birds are captive-bred and trained specifically for public interaction, ensuring both audience safety and bird welfare.

What to Look for When Hiring a Falconer

Not all falconry demonstration providers are equal. Here's what to verify before booking:

  • USDA exhibition permit — Required by federal law for any public display of raptors. Ask to see the permit number.
  • Liability insurance — Professional providers carry general liability coverage. Most venues require a certificate of insurance.
  • State falconry permit — The handler should hold a valid state falconry license at the General or Master level.
  • Experience with public events — Birds used in demonstrations need specific temperament and training. Ask how many events they've done.
  • Safety protocols — A professional will assess the venue, establish a flight perimeter, and brief the audience on behavior expectations.
  • Bird welfare standards — The birds should appear healthy, well-manned, and comfortable around crowds. Multiple birds should be rotated to avoid stress.

Typical Costs

Pricing for falconry demonstrations varies by region, event type, duration, and number of birds:

  • Small private experience (1–6 people): $150–$500
  • Birthday party or small group: $300–$800
  • School assembly or classroom visit: $250–$600
  • Corporate event: $500–$2,500
  • Film/photography (half day): $1,000–$3,000+
  • Wedding flyover or ring delivery: $500–$1,500

Travel fees may apply for events outside the falconer's local area. Most providers offer custom quotes based on your specific needs.

Safety and Legal Requirements

Falconry demonstrations are regulated at both the federal and state level. Key requirements include:

  • The falconer must hold a USDA Class C Exhibitor license for any paid public display.
  • State wildlife agencies may have additional permitting requirements for exhibition.
  • Indoor venues need adequate ceiling height and controlled exits. Outdoor venues need a clear flight area away from roads and power lines.
  • Audience members should not approach birds without handler guidance. Flash photography policies vary by provider.
  • The falconer is responsible for the birds at all times. Guests should follow all handler instructions.
